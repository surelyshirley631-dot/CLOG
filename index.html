        Golden Rules

        Coffee Grinding: The Golden Rules (App Integration Logic)

        1. Fundamental System Constraints
        • The 15-Minute Rule: 60% of flavor compounds dissipate within 15 minutes of grinding. App logic: Trigger a "Grind now" reminder before brewing.
        • Uniformity Over Fineness: Prioritize burr consistency (Flat vs. Conical) over sheer grind size. Inconsistent particles cause muddled extraction (simultaneous bitter/sour notes).
        • Thermal Stability: Friction from continuous grinding heats the burrs, accelerating oil release. Logic: Suggest a +0.5 coarseness offset for back-to-back brewing sessions.
        2. Origin-Specific Grinding Logic
        • Ethiopian (High Density / High Altitude / Heirloom): High fines, risk of clogging filters. Adjustment: +0.5 to +1.0 click (Coarser). Goal: Maintain flow rate and prevent choking.
        • Colombian (Medium Density / Standard Baseline): Baseline bean. Adjustment: 0 (Neutral). Goal: Balanced extraction; use as calibration for new grinders.
        3. Roast Degree Compensation (Dynamic Offset)
        • Light Roast: Low solubility. Strategy: -0.5 to -1.0 click (Finer) to increase surface area.
        • Dark Roast: High porosity and solubility. Strategy: +1.0 to +2.0 clicks (Coarser) to prevent burnt bitterness.
        4. Post-Brew Feedback Loop (Troubleshooting)
        • Flow Rate: Draw-down too slow -> Suggest Coarser (+1). Too fast -> Suggest Finer (-1).
        • Sensory: Astringent/Bitter -> Over-extracted -> Increase grind size. Sour/Thin -> Under-extracted -> Decrease grind size.
